# EcoSat — Final Evidence Sheet

**AESS Sustainability Hackathon 2026 — Challenge 1: Sustainable Electronics for Space Systems**
Author: Ahmed Elsheikh (ahmed.elsheikh@ejust.edu.eg) · Date: 2026-07-03
Repository: <https://github.com/Ahmedvini/EcoSat-Sustainable-FPGA-CubeSat>

## Problem

Conventional small-satellite electronics keep the MCU, sensors, radio, and bus
logic powered for the entire orbit regardless of mission state. In a 95-minute
LEO orbit with a 38-minute eclipse, this wastes stored energy, deepens battery
discharge every orbit, and shortens the usable life of the satellite.

## Core function (the one stable function we demonstrate)

Orbit-aware duty cycling of a modular CubeSat electronics subsystem: the OBC
switches hot-swappable modules (power, sensing, comms, optional FPGA
accelerator) between **active / low-rate / sleep / off** power modes as the
satellite moves between sunlight and eclipse, with module discovery and
hot-swap handled by the PMEP plug-and-play enumeration protocol.

## Baseline (performs the same task)

A monolithic always-on stack executing the same sensing + telemetry mission
for the full orbit. All loads are datasheet supply currents at the 3.3 V rail.

## Results

Generated by `python3 simulation/main.py` (deterministic, no random inputs):

| Scenario | Avg power | Energy/orbit | Energy saved |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Baseline always-on | 242.55 mW | 384.04 mWh | — |
| **Modular duty-cycled (main claim)** | **126.54 mW** | **200.35 mWh** | **47.83%** |
| MCU + FPGA burst (extension scenario) | 70.41 mW | 111.49 mWh | 70.97% |
| Safe mode, low battery (operating case) | 27.28 mW | 43.20 mWh | — |

The FPGA burst case is presented as an extension because it additionally
assumes on-board compression shortens radio active time from 57 to 6 minutes.
The safe-mode case is a policy-robustness check, not a savings claim: below
35% battery SOC the scheduler refuses FPGA activation and limits comms to a
3-minute beacon. The same run renders all plots into `results/graphs/`.

## Test conditions

- Orbit: 550 km sun-synchronous LEO; 95.0 min period = 57.0 min sunlight
  + 38.0 min eclipse.
- Electrical: 3.3 V regulated system rail; battery 7.4 V / 2000 mAh (14.8 Wh).
- Load currents taken from component datasheets (STM32L496, CC1120, TMP117,
  ADXL345, INA3221, Spartan FPGA), listed per phase in
  `results/csv/scenario_phase_power.csv`.
- Tools: Python 3 (stdlib-only core model), Icarus Verilog for RTL testbenches,
  GCC for the PMEP protocol demo.
- RTL implementation: Vivado 2025.2, out-of-context, two targets — ZCU106
  (Zynq UltraScale+ XCZU7EV, development) and Zynq-7010 (28 nm Spartan-class
  fabric, flight reference). 44 LUTs / 90 FFs, timing met at 100 MHz, ~1–2 mW
  dynamic; reports in `rtl/synthesis_reports/`.

## Net-savings accounting (control and decision-logic overhead)

The OBC currents used in the optimized scenarios — 12.0 mA active, 2.1 mA
low-power — are the STM32L496 running the mission scheduler and PMEP decision
logic; control overhead is **inside** the reported numbers, not additional to
them. Power-monitoring current (INA3221) is likewise charged in both baseline
and optimized cases, so the savings are net figures.

## Primary technical KPI

**47.83% electrical energy reduction per orbit** (384.04 → 200.35 mWh;
242.55 → 126.54 mW average) for the conservative duty-cycled design.

## Primary sustainability KPI

**Battery wear**: equivalent full charge cycles drop from ≈143.6 to ≈74.9 per
year (−47.8%), a ≈91.7% extension of cycle-limited battery life — the wear
mechanism most likely to end a CubeSat mission. Secondary effect: average
thermal dissipation falls ~48%, easing thermal design margins.

## Documented limitation

Mode transitions are modeled as instantaneous: inrush and settling energy
during rail switching is not charged, and eclipse duration is fixed at 38 min
rather than season/beta-angle dependent. Both effects are small relative to
the phase-level savings but bound the precision of the headline figure.
